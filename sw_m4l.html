Softwares & M4L
Here are the softwares and Ableton Max4Live (M4L) devices I use to make launchpad covers

Ableton Live 10 Suite is a most powerful edition of live. It provides audio feedback of covers and includes the availability of Max4Live (M4L) devices to expand it's functionality. To play my projects it's not neccesary, but I use some M4Ls just for convinience reasons (like resetting multi devices). Also worth mentioning, this is a paid software. Every launchpad came and comes with Live 10 lite, which is not able to play most of my projects, but you don't have to buy, you can play them using the trial version of Live 10 Suite.
I have to use it as a pirated software. You guys, if you can, buy it. Don't pirate any software, they may come with viruses!

Get it on Ableton.com

Apollo Studio is an independently developed light feedback engine for covers made by mat1jaczyyy and his team. It handles light effects better, like smoother fades and layers based pattern editor. Also adds support to all of the usable colors on the launchpad. It supports Launchpad MK2, Pro, X, Pro MK3. It's activly developed and updated. It can expand it's functionality using the built-in Apollo Connector to connect it to Ableton Live Suite.

Get it from the official site

Apollo Connector is a M4L device used to connect with Apollo Stuido. It comes preinstalled inside Apollo's folder, under M4L.

Input Manager is developed by Kaskobi. It alows you to control projects' behvaiour by choosing your pad midi controller type (Launchpads, MF64, Matrix, etc) and mapping some of the top row buttons (or what you choose) to trigger actions like stopping sound inside Ableton, clearing the launchpad from any MIDIExt generated light effect and resetting multi devices (incl. Kaskobi's & mat1jaczyyy's one).

Get it from Kaskobi.com

Multi Sample is developed again by Kaskobi, allowing you painless multi-sampling without any randoms. Can be reset using Input Manager. A good alternative is Multi by mat1jaczyyy.

Get it from Kaskobi.com
