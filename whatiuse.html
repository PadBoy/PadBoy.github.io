What I use?
Here you will find what softwares and M4L devices I use to create launchpad covers.

  • Launchpad softwares

  • Other softwares

  • Ableton Live 10 Suite
    Ableton Live is the audio provider of the covers. It is a complete DAW, not only for launchpadding. I use the Suite version, as I need Max4Live (M4L) support. Every launchpad comes with Live Lite, which lacks the M4L devices support.

    Purchase on Ableton.com

  • Apollo Studio
    Apollo Stduio provides the light feedback for the covers. It's a completely independently developed piece of software made by mat1jaczyyy and his team. It features more control over your lightshow, smoother animations, and the use of all the available colors on the supported launchpads (MK2, PRO MK1, X, PRO MK3). Also includes Apollo Connector tu use the software with Ableton integration.

    Get from the official website

  • Input Manager
    This M4L device is developed by Kaskobi. It allows you to control your project better by choosing the appropriate input and output device (in Output Manager) to display the launchpad cover properly including Top Lights, Mode light. etc. Also you can map certain buttons to trigger features like stopping audio and (MIDIExt based) light effect feedback, as well as resetting multi plugins (incl. his and mat1jaczyyy's Multi).

    Get from Kaskobi.com

  • Input Manager
    This M4L device is developed by Kaskobi. Multi Sample is used for painless multismapling with no randoms or transposing/pitching. If you don't have Live Suite, you can use the builtin Random device, but it's not resettable and lacks of features like track affecting.

    Get from Kaskobi.com

  • Adobe Premiere Pro CC 2018
    Adobe Premiere Pro is my go to video editor. In the early days I used to use Sony Vegas and Filmora, but Premiere is so convinient for me. I add the special effects and transitions here.

    Purchase on Adobe.com

  • Adobe Photoshop CC 2017
    Adobe Premiere Pro is favourite image editor. I used paint.net nad Affinity Photo, but Photoshop seemed to be the best fre my needs. I make my thumbnails with it.

    Purchase on Adobe.com

  • Davinci Resolve 16
    Davinci Resolve is a coloring/video editor software used in the film industries. It has a free and a paid version with more functionalities. I fix my off-recorded colors of the videos nad adjust the colors of the launchpad.

    Purchase on Blackmagicdesgn.com
